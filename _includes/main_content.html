The 2nd Mapathon @ AAG

April 6–8, Boston

Join us to support humanitarian efforts and map during the AAG Conference

This is the 2nd year that Mapathons and Mapathon related activities will be hosted at AAG. Conference attendees can join a community of online mappers and contribute to OpenStreetMap for humanitarian efforts. Using satellite imagery and freely available OpenStreetMap editing platforms, participants will trace, edit, and label key infrastructure (buildings, roads, etc.), environmental features, and other objects for the creation of openly available real data to produce maps that assist humanitarian and community efforts.

The Mapathon events will feature a wide array of impactful speakers from academia, government, and the private sector. The first Mapathon on Friday afternoon will be hosted on the MIT campus, with mapping projects supporting the Presidential Emergency Plan for Aids Relief (PEPFAR). The Mapathon on Saturday afternoon will be held in the AAG Mapathon lounge and will feature select mapping projects in South America. All Mapathon events are open to the public, you just need to RSVP.

The Mapathons will promote themes of shared humanitarian interest, and mapping will be coordinated through the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team’s Tasking Manager. Recognizing the power that Mapathons have to educate, engage, and empower the public, the organizers hope that at the end of the conference, participants will have made significant contributions towards improved maps to support humanitarian response and sustainable development efforts. For more information about the Mapathons and related AAG activities, please e-mail MapGive@state.gov.

The core organizers of the Mapathon @ AAG include the U.S. Department of State's Office of the Geographer and Global Issues, USAID's GeoCenter, and George Mason University's Department of Geography and GeoInformation Science.
